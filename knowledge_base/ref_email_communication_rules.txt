================================================================================
REFERENCE: EMAIL COMMUNICATION RULES FOR UW DECISIONS
================================================================================
Scope: AI-generated customer email drafts for Decline, Postpone, Counter-offer
Source: AI_Brain_Test_Case_2_Student_One_Page_Guide, Grading Rubric, Sample I/O
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document defines rules and constraints for AI-generated customer emails
that communicate underwriting (UW) decisions. The AI does NOT make decisions —
it only communicates decisions already made by the underwriter.
All generated emails are DRAFTS for human review and must NEVER be auto-sent.

2. DECISION TYPES AND TONE REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Decision Type    | Tone           | Regulatory Level | Decision Finality
-----------------|----------------|------------------|------------------
Decline          | Empathetic     | High             | Final
Postpone         | Reassuring     | High             | Temporary
Counter-offer    | Supportive     | High             | Conditional

3. MANDATORY DISCLOSURE RULES (MUST NOT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The AI MUST NOT disclose or reference:
- Specific medical conditions, diagnoses, or disease names
- Test names, lab results, or medical examination findings
- ICD codes or medical classification codes
- Internal underwriting logic, thresholds, or rules
- Internal system names (L400, DotSphere, FileNet)
- Specific risk factors or risk scoring
- Names of underwriters or internal staff
- Specific SA amounts or premium calculations

The AI MUST NOT:
- Blame the customer or sound judgmental
- Use clinical, technical, or medical language
- Sound robotic, impersonal, or system-generated
- Make promises about future outcomes
- Provide medical advice or health recommendations
- Suggest the customer can appeal or overturn the decision (unless explicitly allowed)

4. MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS (MUST)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The AI MUST:
- Be empathetic, respectful, and dignified
- Use clear, plain language a customer can understand
- Acknowledge the customer's effort in submitting the application
- Provide clear next steps where applicable
- Include customer service contact information
- Maintain a trained frontliner voice (not system/robot voice)
- Keep the email concise and readable
- Explain decisions at principle level only (not detail level)

5. STANDARD EMAIL STRUCTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All customer emails follow this structure:

A. Greeting
   "Dear [Customer Name],"

B. Acknowledgement
   Thank the customer for their time, effort, and patience.
   Show that the application was reviewed carefully.

C. Decision Communication
   State the outcome in general, non-clinical terms.
   - Decline: "we are unable to offer coverage at this time"
   - Postpone: "we require additional clarification before proceeding"
   - Counter-offer: "we are able to offer coverage with adjusted terms"
   Reference "overall assessment" and "current underwriting guidelines" only.

D. Reassurance
   Assure the customer the decision was made carefully.
   Express understanding that the outcome may be disappointing.

E. Next Steps
   - Decline: Offer to explore alternative options
   - Postpone: Explain that a new application can be submitted when ready
   - Counter-offer: Explain the adjusted terms clearly

F. Support Offer
   Direct to customer service team for questions or assistance.

G. Warm Closing
   "Warm regards,"
   "[Frontliner Name / Customer Service Team]"
   "[Company Name]"

6. AS-IS vs TO-BE COMPARISON
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AS-IS (Production emails — PROBLEMS):
- Disclose medical conditions: "Diabetes Mellitus complicated with incipient
  Nephropathy and Atrial Fibrillation on long standing medications"
- Clinical/technical language: "persistent microalbuminuria", "blood thinning
  medication", "irregular heartbeat medication"
- Distressing and overwhelming for the customer
- Written from underwriter perspective, not customer perspective
- High complaint risk and PDPA/regulatory risk

TO-BE (AI-generated emails — TARGET):
- No medical details disclosed
- Empathetic, warm, frontliner voice
- Clear and concise
- Customer feels respected even when outcome is negative
- Compliant with regulatory requirements
- Consistent across all communications

VALUE CREATED:
| Dimension        | AS-IS       | TO-BE          | Value                   |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Regulatory Risk | High        | Low            | Reduced MAS complaint   |
| Customer Emotion| Distress    | Reassured      | TNPS protection         |
| Tone            | Clinical    | Human          | Trust                   |
| Consistency     | Varies      | Standardised   | Scalable CX             |
| Frontliner Effort| Manual     | AI-assisted    | Productivity            |

8. INPUT PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Required inputs for email generation:
| Parameter          | Type   | Description                           |
|-------------------|--------|---------------------------------------|
| decision_type      | string | Decline / Postpone / CounterOffer     |
| customer_name      | string | Customer's name for greeting          |
| outcome_summary    | string | General outcome (no medical details)  |
| tone_required      | string | Empathetic / Reassuring / Supportive  |
| channel            | string | Advisory / Direct                     |
| language           | string | English (default)                     |

Optional inputs:
| Parameter              | Type    | Description                        |
|-----------------------|---------|------------------------------------|
| acknowledge_effort     | boolean | Include effort acknowledgement     |
| support_offer_required | boolean | Include support contact info       |
| decision_finality      | string  | Final / Temporary / Conditional    |

9. OUTPUT FORMAT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The AI must produce two sections:

Section 1: Analysis (internal, for ops review)
- Decision context and tone assessment
- Guardrails check (confirm no medical disclosure)
- Confidence flags

Section 2: Customer Email (for human review before sending)
- Subject line
- Full email body following the standard structure

10. KEY PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"The decision does not change — only the customer's experience of the decision
does. A well-written message can preserve trust even when the outcome is negative."
