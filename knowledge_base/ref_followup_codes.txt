====================================================================
CRITICAL FOLLOW-UP CODES — DETAILED BUSINESS DEFINITIONS
====================================================================

These codes have specific decision-path implications in the SOP:

CSL — Customer Clarification Required
  Trigger: Raised when customer information is incomplete or needs verification
  Action to resolve: Customer must provide clarification or missing info
  Impact on decision: If status = O (Outstanding) → Further Requirements
  Frequency: Appears on nearly every QnB CareShield case in UAT data
  Resolution: Status changes to R when clarification received and verified
  Who resolves: Ops processor (with customer response)

C09 — Compliance / GNS Re-screening Required
  Trigger: Raised when Government Name Screening (GNS) check is needed,
           OR when client fields are amended that require compliance re-check
  Action to resolve: GNS screening must be completed
  Impact on decision: If status = O → Trigger GNS Review (Decision D)
                      If GNS hit found → Trigger Compliance (Decision E)
  Resolution: Status changes to R when GNS screening completes clean
  Who resolves: Compliance team
  IMPORTANT: C09 = O drives a DIFFERENT decision path than other FUP codes.
             Other codes with O → Further Requirements, but C09 with O → GNS/Compliance

F45 — Premium Mismatch Detected
  Trigger: System detects premium amount discrepancy between proposal and L400
  Action to resolve: Investigate cause (birthday crossover, gender/DOB error,
                     campaign code, backdating needed)
  Impact on decision: If status = O → Further Requirements (premium analysis needed)
  Frequency: 53 occurrences in NB.MASTER (most common FUP code)
  Resolution: Status changes to R when premium is reconciled
  Who resolves: Ops processor (may need system correction)

AT3 — Additional Requirement Pending
  Trigger: Additional documentation or information required from customer/advisor
  Action to resolve: Specified additional requirements must be fulfilled
  Impact on decision: If status = O → Further Requirements
  Resolution: Status changes to R when requirement is met
  Who resolves: Ops processor (with customer/advisor response)

====================================================================
EVALUATION RULES
====================================================================

When evaluating follow-up codes for a case:
- ALL follow-up codes must have status "R" (Resolved) for the case to proceed
- Any non-R status means the case has outstanding items
- F45 (Premium) is the most common — check premium reconciliation
- UWO/UW2-UW6 indicate underwriting is not complete
- APS/MED/MEN/ECG indicate medical requirements outstanding
- Multiple codes on a single policy indicate complex cases requiring manual review
